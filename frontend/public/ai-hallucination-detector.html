AI Hallucination Detector for Academic Citations

With the rise of AI writing assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini, a new problem has emerged: AI-generated fake citations that don't exist in reality.

What Are AI Citation Hallucinations?

When AI language models generate academic references, they often create citations that:

  • Look perfectly formatted and legitimate
  • Include real author names and journal titles
  • Have plausible publication years and page numbers
  • But don't actually exist
Example AI Hallucination:
Smith, J. K., & Johnson, L. M. (2023). "The Impact of Digital Transformation on Academic Research Methods." Journal of Research Innovation, 45(3), 234-251.

This citation looks real but is completely fabricated by AI.

Why Do AI Models Generate Fake References?

AI language models:

  • Are trained on patterns, not factual databases
  • Generate text based on probability, not truth
  • Can't verify if a citation actually exists
  • Mix real author names with fictional papers
