Method 2: Manual Verification (Time-Consuming)

  1. Search the exact title in Google Scholar
  2. Verify author names match exactly
  3. Check publication year and journal volume
  4. Confirm page numbers are within range
  5. Test DOI links if provided
Warning: Manual checking takes 5-10 minutes per citation and may miss subtle fakes.

Red Flags: Signs of Fake Citations

Too Perfect Formatting

AI often generates citations with suspiciously round page numbers (100-125, 50-75)

Recent Publication Dates

Fake citations often claim to be from 2021-2024, especially for older topics

Generic Author Names

Common names like "Smith, J." or "Johnson, M." paired with prestigious journals

Plausible But Vague Titles

Titles that sound academic but are too broad or generic for the claimed journal
